Shadow Board Generator

ArUco-guided silhouette → laser-cut SVG

1 How it works

  1. ADownload and print the datum sheet below at 100% scale (no "fit to page"). Place one or more sheets on the same flat surface as your tools — they don't need to be in any special arrangement, just visible in the photo.
  2. BLay your tools flat on the surface. Take a photo roughly from above. The more overhead the shot, the better — but the app corrects for moderate angles.
  3. CUpload the photo on the Process tab. The app detects the printed ArUco markers, corrects perspective, extracts the tool silhouettes, and produces a scaled SVG ready for your laser cutter.

2 Download datum sheet

The sheet contains ArUco markers at a precisely known size. Print it at 100% scale on any printer. You can cut the sheet into individual markers if you want to scatter them around large tool arrangements.

⚠ After printing, verify one marker measures the specified size with a ruler. If your printer scaled it, note the actual printed size and enter it on the Process tab.

Tips for best results

  • Use a light, matte, uniform surface (foam, light wood, paper on table) for dark metal tools.
  • For light-coloured tools, toggle "Light tools on dark background" on the Process tab.
  • Avoid strong shadows or glare — diffuse lighting works best.
  • Keep the camera roughly parallel to the surface (low angle causes heavy distortion).
  • Place at least one marker per photo. More markers improve accuracy and robustness.
  • Markers can be on separate cut pieces scattered around the tools — they don't need to be on the same sheet or precisely placed.
